[INTERVIEWER]: Here's the problem statement on the screen. Design a system that ingests electricity readings from smart meters and lets customers view usage and estimated cost in a mobile or web app.

Functional requirements:
- Ingest half-hourly meter readings from an external metering provider.
- Associate each reading with the correct meter, property, and customer account.
- Support readings that arrive late, arrive out of chronological order, are duplicated, or are subsequently corrected.
- Customers can retrieve usage for the last 24 hours, daily usage for the last 30 days, and monthly usage for the last 24 months.
- Calculate estimated cost using the tariff effective at consumption time.
- Internal support staff can inspect raw and processed readings for an account.
- Reprocess historical readings when calculation logic changes.

Scale:
- 1 million active customers.
- Up to 2 meters per customer.
- Approximately 50 million readings per day.
- Providers may deliver bursts up to 20x average ingestion rate.
- Customers generate 5,000 dashboard requests per second at peak.

Non-functional requirements:
- No accepted meter reading may be silently lost.
- Processing must be idempotent.
- New readings should appear within five minutes of being received.
- Usage-query latency below 300 ms p95.
- Customer-facing availability target 99.9%.
- Customer data must be isolated and access auditable.

Out of scope:
- Legally binding invoices.
- Payment collection.
- Direct communication with physical smart meters.
